# Survey Question Analysis
# Question-by-question redundancy audit + strategic value assessment

## OVERLAP MAP

Questions that ask the SAME or near-same thing are grouped. Only one from each group survives.

---

## GROUP A — "What do you wish people knew about you?" / "What do you want visible?"

**Q1:** "What's the one thing you wish they already knew about you?" ← **SURVIVE**
**Q13:** "If reputation was portable, what's the one thing you'd want agencies to see first?" ← **REDUNDANT** — same as Q1
**Q4:** "What one thing would you change about how agencies/families verify you?" ← **REDUNDANT** — asks the same pain differently

*Decision: Q1 is the cleanest version. Q4 is useful as a B-answer but Q1 covers it.*

---

## GROUP B — "What's the hardest part of switching / starting from zero?"

**Q2:** "What was the hardest part of your last agency/family transition?" ← **SURVIVE**
**Q12:** "How frustrated are you when asked to re-verify what you've already proven?" ← **REDUNDANT** — same pain, specific scenario

*Decision: Q2 is more open, more emotionally revealing. Keep Q2. Q12 is implied.*

---

## GROUP C — "Have you lost opportunities due to inability to prove your track record?"

**Q3:** "Have you ever lost a job because you couldn't prove your track record fast enough?" ← **SURVIVE**
**Q7:** "Have you ever felt your best work was invisible to the system?" ← **REDUNDANT** — emotional framing of the same problem

*Decision: Q3 is a concrete yes/no that gives us market sizing data. Q7 is less measurable.*

---

## GROUP D — "What are you proud of / what should you be recognized for?"

**Q6:** "What's something you did that was never formally recognized?" ← **SURVIVE**
**Q8:** "What makes you feel proud of your work?" ← **REDUNDANT** — same territory

*Decision: Q6 is more emotionally loaded, reveals a specific frustration. Q8 is too soft.*

---

## GROUP E — "What matters most on your profile?"

**Q9:** "What matters most on your profile? (top 3)" ← **SURVIVE**
**Q4** already covered above (moved to redundancy)

*Decision: Q9 is the product design question — this is one to keep.*

## STILL TOO MANY — My recommendation for the FINAL 10

*For the landing page survey — must be completable in 3 minutes*

1. **"What's the hardest part about being recognized for what you can actually do?"** (Q1 refined) — emotional hook
2. **"Have you ever lost a job because you couldn't prove your track record fast enough?"** (Q3) — yes/no, validates pain
3. **"What's the hardest part of your last agency transition?"** (Q2) — specific, concrete
4. **"What matters most on your caregiver profile? (top 3)"** (Q9) — product design
5. **"What would make you proud to share your caregiver ID?"** (Q10) — ID design
6. **"Would you feel more in control of your career if your reputation was portable?"** (Q17) — value prop validation
7. **"What would make you recommend this to a fellow caregiver?"** (Q20) — referral strategy
8. **"Which badges matter most? (top 2)"** (Q18) — badge design
9. **"What would you never put on a public profile?"** (Q16) — privacy/product limits
10. **Email** (Q26) — waitlist capture

These 10 give you:
- Emotional validation (Q1, Q2, Q3)
- Product design (Q4, Q5, Q8, Q9)
- Value prop confirmation (Q6)
- Acquisition strategy (Q7)
- Email capture (Q10)

Everything else goes to the post-signup long-form survey.
